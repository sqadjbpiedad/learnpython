Exercise 2: Total Population Calculation¶

Topic: Adding Arrays

Description: Add two lists of numbers together effectively.

Task: You have the counts of Males and Females for 5 different barangays. Add them together to get the Total Population for each barangay.

  • Males: [100, 150, 120, 130, 110]

  • Females: [110, 140, 130, 120, 115]

np_male = np.array([100, 150, 120, 130, 110])
np_female = np.array([110, 140, 130, 120, 115])

total_population_per_bgy = np_male + np_female
print(f'Total population of each barangay: {total_population_per_bgy}')

# Total population of all barangays
total_population = np.sum(total_population_per_bgy)
print(f'Total population of each barangay: {total_population}')

# Average population male and female
ave_male = np.mean(np_male)
print(f'Average population of male: {ave_male}')
ave_female = np.mean(np_female)
print(f'Average population of female: {ave_female}')

# Difference of barangay population from the average

Exercise 3: Finding the Highest Inflation Rate¶

Topic: np.max

Description: Find the largest number in a list.

Task: Given a list of inflation rates from different months, identify the highest rate recorded using np.max.

  • Rates: [3.4, 4.2, 5.1, 8.7, 6.1, 4.5]

# Write solution here
inflation_rates = np.array([3.4, 4.2, 5.1, 8.7, 6.1, 4.5])

highest_inflation = np.max(inflation_rates)
print(f'Highest inflation: {highest_inflation}')
Highest inflation: 8.7

Exercise 5: Filter Large Households¶

Topic: Filtering (Boolean Indexing)

Description: Find numbers that are bigger than a specific value.

Task: You have a list of household family sizes. Find and print only the family sizes that have more than 5 members.

  • Family Sizes: [3, 4, 8, 2, 6, 10, 4]

# Write solution here

Long Exercise: Annual Report on Violence Against Women (VAW)¶

Scenario: You are preparing a report on the Monthly Reported Cases of Violence Against Women (VAW) in a specific municipality for the year 2024.

Your Tasks:

  1. Create the Data: Convert the list of 12 months into a NumPy array.

  2. Total Annual Cases: Calculate the total number of VAW cases reported for the whole year.

  3. Average Monthly Cases: Calculate the average number of cases reported per month.

  4. Peak Incident Month: Identify the highest number of cases recorded in a single month.

  5. Identify High-Risk Months: Find and print all months where the reported cases were greater than 20 (indicating a surge).

Raw Data (Jan-Dec): 15, 18, 12, 14, 25, 28, 10, 8, 30, 22, 15, 19

data = [
    {"month": "January", "case": 15},
    {"month": "February", "case": 18},
    {"month": "March", "case": 12},
    {"month": "April", "case": 14},
    {"month": "May", "case": 25},
    {"month": "June", "case": 28},
    {"month": "July", "case": 10},
    {"month": "August", "case": 8},
    {"month": "September", "case": 30},
    {"month": "October", "case": 22},
    {"month": "November", "case": 15},
    {"month": "December", "case": 19}
]

months = np.array([month['month'] for month in data])
incidents = np.array([incident['case'] for incident in data])

total_annual_case = np.sum(incidents)
print(f'Total Annual Cases: {total_annual_case}')

average_monthly_cases = np.mean(incidents)
print(f'Average Monthly Cases: {average_monthly_cases}')

peak_incident_month = np.max(incidents)
print(f'Peak Incident Month: {peak_incident_month}')

high_risk_months = months[incidents > 20]
print(f'High Risk Months: {high_risk_months}')
Total Annual Cases: 216
Average Monthly Cases: 18.0
Peak Incident Month: 30
High Risk Months: ['May' 'June' 'September' 'October']
